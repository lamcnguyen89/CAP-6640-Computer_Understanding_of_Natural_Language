Introduction to Module 6
Module Introduction
Main Focus: This module examines advanced language understanding through coreference resolution, unified multi-task learning, and semantic composition. It begins with entity tracking and coreference resolution, analyzing mention detection, mention-pair and mention-ranking models, clustering approaches, and end-to-end neural systems. It then transitions to unified and multi-task learning frameworks that recast diverse NLP tasks as question answering problems, culminating in architectures such as DecaNLP and MQAN that eliminate task-specific heads and enable transfer and zero-shot generalization.

Secondary Focus: The module further explores semantic composition and structured meaning, including compositionality, recursion, phrase representation in vector spaces, TreeRNN and SU-RNN architectures, and scene parsing across NLP and vision. Students analyze how meaning emerges hierarchically from lexical units, how syntactic ambiguity (e.g., PP attachment) affects interpretation, and how modern neural architectures integrate structural reasoning with contextual embeddings. The module connects linguistic theory, neural modeling, and multimodal scene understanding to build a principled foundation for scalable language understanding systems.

Module Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:

Explain the coreference resolution pipeline, including mention detection, feature extraction, and clustering.
Compare mention-pair, mention-ranking, clustering-based, and end-to-end neural coreference models.
Analyze the role of knowledge-based and neural contextual embeddings in entity tracking.
Explain unified multi-task learning and QA-based reformulations of NLP tasks.
Describe DecaNLP and MQAN architectures, including pointer mechanisms and anti-curriculum training.
Evaluate zero-shot classification through question-pointer reformulations.
Explain semantic composition, recursion, and hierarchical structure in language understanding.
Compare phrase representation methods: averaging, RNNs, Transformers, and Tree-based models.
Analyze structural ambiguity (e.g., PP attachment) and its implications for parsing.
Assess multimodal scene parsing approaches integrating NLP and vision.

Introduction to Module 2
Module Introduction
Main Focus: This module develops the core modeling foundations required to move from word-level representations to structured and sequential language understanding. It begins by formalizing neural modeling for NLP tasks, focusing on window-based classification, max-margin objectives, and the optimization of neural networks through backpropagation. Students examine how context windows, scoring functions, and loss formulations enable structured prediction tasks such as named entity recognition, while also confronting practical pitfalls in representation learning, particularly the consequences of retraining word embeddings on limited data.

Secondary Focus: Building on neural optimization, the module transitions to the grammatical structure of language, introducing phrase structure, context-free grammars, and dependency-based representations. Students explore how syntactic structure enables precise interpretation, why ambiguity arises in natural language, and how parsing algorithms resolve competing structural analyses. The module culminates in modern parsing approaches, contrasting rule-based grammars with data-driven and neural parsers, and highlighting the role of annotated treebanks and deep learning in scalable, robust syntactic analysis.

Module Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:

Explain window-based neural models for NLP classification tasks
Describe max-margin loss functions and their role in structured prediction
Explain backpropagation and gradient-based optimization in neural NLP models
Identify pitfalls of retraining word embeddings on limited data
Describe phrase structure and context-free grammars
Explain syntactic ambiguity and its impact on language interpretation
Differentiate between constituency-based and dependency-based parsing
Describe dependency grammar and dependency tree representations
Explain the role of treebanks and annotated data in parsing
Describe neural and deep learning–based parsing approaches

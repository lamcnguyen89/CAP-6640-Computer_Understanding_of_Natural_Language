Introduction to Module 7
Module Introduction
Main Focus: This module provides a comprehensive examination of modern Large Language Models (LLMs), including their architectural foundations, training paradigms, alignment strategies, optimization techniques, and deployment ecosystems. Students will analyze the core model families—autoregressive, autoencoding, encoder-decoder, retrieval-augmented, multimodal, and instruction-aligned systems—along with their respective pretraining objectives such as causal language modeling, masked language modeling, denoising, and contrastive learning. The module further explores alignment mechanisms including instruction tuning, RLHF, and DPO, as well as large-scale optimization strategies such as parallelism, mixed precision, and curriculum learning.

Secondary Focus: Beyond architecture and training, the module critically examines reasoning and generalization in language models. Students will investigate the cognitive gap between humans and machines, few-shot generalization, out-of-distribution robustness, explainability, and informal deductive reasoning. Emphasis is placed on Chain-of-Thought prompting, tool-augmented reasoning, and the debate surrounding whether LLMs truly reason or merely simulate reasoning behavior. The module concludes with open challenges in interpretability, reliability, symbolic integration, and evaluation of reasoning competence.

Module Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:

Differentiate between autoregressive, autoencoding, encoder-decoder, retrieval-augmented, and multimodal LLM architectures.
Explain core pretraining objectives: CLM, MLM, denoising, and contrastive learning.
Describe alignment methods including instruction tuning, RLHF, and DPO.
Analyze optimization and scaling techniques such as data/model parallelism, FSDP/ZeRO, and mixed precision.
Evaluate open-source vs. closed-source commercial LLM ecosystems.
Explain the cognitive differences between humans and machines in learning and reasoning.
Analyze few-shot learning and out-of-distribution robustness challenges.
Assess Chain-of-Thought prompting and related reasoning-enhancement techniques.
Critically evaluate claims of reasoning ability in large language models.
Identify open research challenges in generalization, interpretability, and reasoning reliability.

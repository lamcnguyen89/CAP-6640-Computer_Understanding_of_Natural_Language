Module Introduction
Main Focus: Module 0 serves as the formal entry point to the course Computer Understanding of Natural Language, establishing the structural, administrative, and conceptual foundations required for the remainder of the semester. The module introduces students to the overall scope of the course, including its intellectual motivation, topical coverage across eight modules, and its positioning relative to prior coursework in natural language processing and machine learning. It clarifies the organization of the course, instructional logistics, communication channels, and expectations for student engagement, while situating the course within a modern, research-driven view of NLP inspired by advanced offerings such as Stanford’s CS224n. The module ensures that all students begin with a shared understanding of what the course is about, how it will unfold over the semester, and what knowledge and effort will be required to succeed. 

Secondary Focus: Beyond logistics, this module emphasizes accountability, rigor, and academic integrity as core values of the course. It provides a detailed walkthrough of attendance requirements, grading structure, assignment policies, exam formats, project expectations, and the strict stance on the use of generative AI tools. Students are also introduced to institutional and classroom policies, including accommodations, ethical conduct, citation standards, and classroom etiquette. By addressing these elements early, Module 0 minimizes ambiguity, reduces downstream friction, and empowers students to plan their workload strategically, select appropriate project topics, and align their efforts with both course-level and university-level expectations. The module concludes by creating space for clarification and questions, ensuring that students enter the technical modules with confidence and clear operational understanding. 

Module Objectives
By the end of this module, students will be able to:

Identify the course structure, schedule, personnel, and communication mechanisms.
Explain the high-level goals and thematic progression of the NLP modules covered throughout the semester.
Understand attendance, grading, assignment, exam, and project policies.
Interpret expectations regarding individual work, academic integrity, and citation practices.
Understand the role, scope, and milestones of the course project.
Recognize institutional policies related to accommodations, ethics, and classroom conduct.
Plan coursework and assessments strategically based on grading weights and deadlines.
